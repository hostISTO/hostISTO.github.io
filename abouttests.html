1. What is an ISTO Test?

The ISTO test as a multiple choice test aimed at establishing the level of understanding of the subject ISO management system standard of the candidate. Starting from 2020, the ISTO tests are primarily delivered electronically as a public service through our network of computer based test centres, or as an inhouse service for corporate customers. 

The ISTO Tests focuses not only on the requirements of the standards, but also ensures that those who pass the tests have demonstrated a knowledge of the underlying principles, the respective definitions and its practical implications.

The test conists of multiple choice test items with four (4) possible answers, and only one (1) ‘best’ answer. Candidates meeting the passing criteria will be awarded a Certificate of Achievement. All candidates will also receive a proprietary analytic report showing the areas of weakness in each the test sections, based on incorrect responses.

The ISTO test is a closed book test. However candidates are allowed to refer to a clean copy of the respective ISO standard during the test. Such copy is the only permitted item during the test.

Candidates achieving the passing criteria will be offered a Certificate of Achievement as a demonstration of their thorough understanding in the respective standards.

2. Significance and Relevance of ISTO tests

Either as a user, or a professional such as the System Responsible Personnel, auditor or consultant, every stakeholder has an important role to play in effective system implementation. In the long run, there will be major difference in outcome and system performance, between an effective management system and a mediocre implementation.

Taking an ISTO test will offer a professional qualification. The proprietary analytic report will point to candidates’ potential areas of improvement.

The ISTO Tests of Understanding are structured as follows:

Section 1: Principles and definitions. Applicability for Level 2 and Level 3.

Section 2: Questions related to clauses 4, 5, 6, 9 and 10

Section 3: Questions related to clauses 7 and 8

Section 4: Implementation (case studies, each case consists of 5 questions)

4. Test Analytics (CREAM Analysis)

Based on ISTO research and endorsed by the ISTO Technical Advisory Board, the level of understanding to an ISO management system standard or other similar standard can be grouped into two categories:

Category 1: the scope and applicability of the management system standard, the management principles and the terms and definitions used.

Category 2: the text of the standards (Clauses 4 to 10 of the ISO Annex L based management system standards)

Such Level of Understandings can be measured in five distinct areas - CREAM.

Candidates can demonstrate knowledge of:

Category 1

A: the Applicability of the standard. This includes the intent of a requirement, and the scope of the standard.

M: the Management principles on which the management system standard was written. This includes the meaning of the terms and definitions and the sequence of activities as required in the management system standard.

Category 2

C: the appropriate Clause reference of a specific requirement.

R: a certain Requirement in the Standard (i.e. the text of the requirement). 
Such a requirement can be related to documentation or not related to documentation.

E: the non Existence of certain requirements (in the standard)
Such non-existence can be related to documentation or not related to documentation.
