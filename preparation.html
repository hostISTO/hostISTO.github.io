1. Getting Prepared

Acquisition of knowledge

There are various ways to acquire the required knowledge for an ISTO test, including but not limited to:

  • Perform individual study
  • Participate in informal study groups
  • Attend formal review courses 

Some keys to success include:

  • Knowing your strengths and weaknesses
  • Having an test preparation plan
  • Developing a test-taking strategy

If a candidate is re-taking an ISTO Test, they should pay special attention to the weaknesses identified in the ISTO Analytical Report. Note that knowledge and understanding are essential in passing the test.

Special assistance

If a candidate requires external assistive devices or equipment such as crutches or wheelchair, the candidate must communicate with the respective Test Centres of your need to use these devices in the secure testing room at least two weeks in advance of the test date. If you fail to inform the ISTO Test Centre accordingly, the candidate may not be permitted to use those devices in the secure testing room.

2. The Night Before

What Do I Need to Bring to the Test Centre?

You must bring valid government-issued photo identification. Acceptable forms of identification include:

  • Identification Card
  • Valid driver’s license
  • Current passport

Note: the spelling of name must be 100% identical to your registration. Any change in certification would incur administration fees.

If you fail to bring the appropriate identification and the Test voucher you will forfeit your test writing.


Directions to the site

Follow the directions or travel advice as given in the Test voucher. In case of doubt, contact the test centre before the test.

Calming your nerves

Part of controlling anxiety involves being prepared for what to expect. Here are some tips:

Prior to the test:

  • Know your material. Spend adequate time studying for the test.
  • Ensure you get enough sleep, good nutrition, exercise, personal down time, etc. Think positively.

On the day of the test

  • Allow yourself plenty of extra time to arrive at the testing centre so you have adequate time to park, register, etc.
  • Dress comfortably - you will be seated for 3 1/2 hours.

During the test

  • Relax. Practice relaxation techniques such as deep-breathing.
  • Do not focus on any anxiety you may be experiencing.
  • Do not expect to know all the answers. It is unlikely that a candidate will answer all questions correctly. Use your reasoning ability to analyze the question and identify the best possible answer.
3. What to expect at the Computer-based Test Centres

Check In

Plan to arrive early for your appointment. If you are 15 or more minutes late for your appointment, you may not be able to write your test and you may forfeit your test writing.

If you are not a citizen of the country in which you are testing, the only acceptable identification document is your valid, unexpired passport.

When you arrive to take the test, you must show proof of your identity to the Test Administrator by providing government-issued photo identification. Candidates without an acceptable photo ID will not be permitted to enter the test area nor will they receive a refund of their test fees. 

The Test Centre will provide to each candidate a copy of the respective ISO management system standard. Candidates should not mark on the standard by any means and should return the copy to the Test Centre after the test is over.



Personal Belongings

All candidates will be required to leave their personal belongings outside the testing area. While the testing centre will make available to you a secure area to store your belongings, the Test Centre and ISTO assumes no responsibility for lost, stolen, broken or misplaced personal property and candidates should plan appropriately.


Before entering the testing area, an unobtrusive visual inspection of personal items will be conducted by the test administrators.


Prohibited items include into the Test Area include

  • Reference materials such as course notes, books etc.
  • All kinds of electronic devices, such as cameras, mobile phones, notebook computers, tablets, recorders of any kind;
  • All forms of outerwear, including but not limited to hats, scarves, gloves, jackets, coats;
  • All forms of luggage, including but not limited to book bags, backpacks, handbags, purses, suitcases and briefcases;
  • Food and beverage;
  • Sharp objects (including weapons) or tools of any kind, including but not limited to sharp objects, firearms, explosive devices, razor blades, screw drivers, hammers, box cutters and knives;
  • Hats or headwear of any kind unless provision has been made for specific religious apparel.

Objects worn, or brought into the testing area, because of the candidates sincerely held personal religious belief, practice or observance will be permitted, subject to prior notification and authorization by the ISTO.

You may access stored medicine or food or drinks outside the secure testing room. You may not access other personal belongings until you complete your test.

Smoking, drinking, or eating is not permitted in the secure test area.



Late arrivals

Candidates should arrive at the writing location no later than 30 minutes prior to the scheduled start time. Unscheduled or late candidates will not be admitted.

Please give yourself ample time to arrive at the test centre location. As many test centres are located within urban areas, parking may be limited. Please plan accordingly. 

If you arrive more than 30 minutes beyond the starting time of your scheduled appointment, or if you fail to present acceptable identification when you arrive for your scheduled appointment, you may be refused admission, and you may forfeit your test writing. 

To sit for the test after having been refused admission, you must register and pay for another test writing. 


Weather conditions 

In the event of unusual weather or unforeseen emergencies on the day of the test, Test Centre Management will determine whether circumstances warrant the cancellation of the test writing. The test will usually not be cancelled if the test centre personnel can open the test centre.


Every attempt will be made to administer tests as scheduled. However, should an test be cancelled by ISTO or the Test Centre, all scheduled candidates will be granted one writing extension if the test is not able to be rescheduled within the writing window.

4. During the Test

The Test Administrator will get you with the Test Home Page. Candidates have to click and agree to the Terms and Conditions, Malpractices Commitment, and Non-Disclosure Agreement before proceeding to the actual test start screen.


The Test Administrator will give positive confirmation for all candidates to start at the same time. The test clock then starts clicking.


During the Test:

  • Take your time reading the questions. If you have a problem with a question, at least try a Tentative Response. Positively you have time to come back to review the question. If not, the Tentative Response will be considered as final response in the marking process.
  • Try to complete all questions, even by guessing (25% chance!). Never leave a question behind.
  • Answer ALL questions. Blanks are always incorrect. There is no penalty for guessing.
  • The Test Administrator cannot answer questions regarding the test. If you think a question has a mistake or is unclear, use the feedback window down below the question. You can state your assumptions.
  • No moving about the room or other behavior distracting to other candidates is permitted.
  • One candidate at a time will be permitted to leave the test area to use the restroom or for emergencies.

If you need to use the washroom, or when you leave your workstation for any reason, you must sign out and sign back in -- the Test Administrator will log your activity. Your test clock continues to run during this time. You are not allowed to leave the building (or part of the building) for any reason while your test clock is running.


Response strategy

Knowing how to take the test will help improve your score. The test uses multiple-choice items with only one (1) correct answer and three (3) incorrect answers. Remember, the goal is to get

as many items correct as possible. There is no penalty for selecting an incorrect answer. However, only correct answers count toward reaching the passing score.

  • Read the items carefully
  • Consider the context
  • Use test time wisely
  • Go back to troublesome items
  • Complete all items• Read the items carefully


Candidate satisfaction survey

After the test is completed, the candidate is invited to provide some feedback on their overall experience, and any suggestions for improvement. Areas include

Awareness of the ISO test

User friendlines of the registration process

User friendlines of the check in process

Technical quality of questions

Practicality of the test questions

ISTO Test of Understanding Overall rating

Qualitative comments (positive and negative)


Emergency Cancellations or Withdrawals

In the case of an emergency, the Test Administrator shall determine if the administration of the test can continue or if the test needs to be ended.


During the test, if a candidate wish to withdraw from the test due to illness, emergency, or other causes, the Test Administrator onsite must document the time of the incident and a description of what occurred.


Following the administration of the test, the administrator must complete an incident report and return it to ISTO Head Office for review.


Test environment and security


The ISTO will endeavor to ensure that the environment for writing the test is acceptable. Please be aware that on occasion, candidates may be exposed to minor distractions and the temperature in the testing area may vary. Candidates who are easily distracted by noise may request earplugs or noise-cancelling headphones from the proctor.


The ISTO and the Test Centre maintain test administration and test security standards that are designed to assure that all candidates are provided the same opportunity to demonstrate their abilities.


The ISTO reserves the sole right to cancel or withhold any test scores if, in its opinion, there is adequate reason to question their validity.


5. After the ISTO Test  

Getting your results


Once the test is completed, the candidate should receive the official notification of test results via regular email generated by the ISTO system typically in one to two hours after the test. Alternatively, the candidate can login their ISTO account to retrieve the results.


Where candidates has commented/remarked on a question and the result of which affects the pass/fail decision, then there may be a delay of few days in the announcement of result. Anyways there will be a notice to the students in one to two hours regarding this slightly delay.


Re-taking the ISTO Test


Should a candidate is not satisfied of his results, or wish to verify if they could improve their areas of weakness in their understanding of the test, they can re-take a test at 50% discount.
